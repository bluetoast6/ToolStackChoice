Make vs n8n: Which automation tool is better for founders in 2026?

Last updated: February 2026 — based on hands-on testing and current pricing.

If you're a founder or part of a small team handling marketing or operations, Make and n8n are probably the two tools you'll hear about first. This page pulls together everything we've learned from testing them, so you can quickly decide which one fits your stack and workflow style.

Scope note

Right now, this guide focuses on Make and n8n — the automation tools we've tested hands-on so far. We'll add Zapier and other tools over time, and update this page as new reviews go live.

Quick recommendations

Choose Make if you want a highly visual, no-code canvas and quick wins without worrying about servers or self-hosting.

Choose n8n if you want maximum flexibility, self-hosting options, and are comfortable with more technical workflows.

What are Make and n8n?

Make is a visual automation platform that lets you connect apps and APIs using drag-and-drop scenarios. It's designed for non-technical users and small teams who want powerful workflows without writing code.

n8n is a workflow automation tool with open-source roots and strong developer appeal. You can use it via n8n Cloud or self-host it, making it attractive if you care about data control, customization, or building very specific automations.

Who each tool is best for

Make is best for you if:

  • You're a non-technical founder, marketer, or ops person.
  • You want to see your workflows visually and build them quickly.
  • You prefer a hosted platform where you don't manage infrastructure.
  • You're happy to trade some low-level control for speed and ease of use.

n8n is best for you if:

  • You or someone on your team is comfortable with APIs, webhooks, and basic coding concepts.
  • You want the option to self-host or customize heavily.
  • You're building complex, logic-heavy automations that may outgrow simpler tools.
  • You care about owning your data and having more control over performance.

Key differences at a glance

  • Ease of use Make is more beginner-friendly, with a cleaner, more visual interface. n8n has a steeper learning curve but more power once you're used to it.
  • Flexibility n8n gives you more control over logic, branching, and custom integrations, especially if you self-host. Make is opinionated but still powerful for most business workflows.
  • Setup and hosting Make is fully hosted. n8n can be used via n8n Cloud or self-hosted on your own infrastructure.
  • Pricing logic Make uses usage-based pricing tied to operations/credits, which can scale quickly with heavy workflows. n8n offers both a free self-hosted edition and paid cloud plans, which can be cost-effective if you run higher-volume workflows efficiently.

When to choose Make

  • You want to automate internal processes, marketing, reporting, or basic operations quickly.
  • You prefer a polished, visual builder that your team can understand at a glance.
  • You're okay paying for a hosted service in exchange for speed and reliability.

When to choose n8n

  • You're building sophisticated workflows with lots of logic, branching, or custom code.
  • You want the option to run the automation engine on your own servers.
  • You're willing to invest more time up front in exchange for long-term flexibility.

How we review automation tools

For automation platforms, we look at:

  • Ease of use for non-technical users.
  • Integration depth and coverage.
  • Flexibility for complex workflows.
  • Pricing fairness and how costs scale with real-world usage.
  • Reliability and how well automations run at scale.

Automation FAQs

Is Make or n8n better for non-technical founders?

Make is usually the easier starting point for non-technical users, thanks to its visual drag-and-drop canvas and polished interface. n8n is better suited to founders or teams who are comfortable with APIs, webhooks, and more technical workflow logic.

Can I switch from Make to n8n later?

There is no one-click migration between the two platforms, so switching means rebuilding your workflows from scratch in n8n. It is realistic to do, but it takes meaningful effort — especially if you have many complex scenarios.

Do I need to self-host n8n?

No. n8n Cloud is a fully hosted option for teams that do not want to manage their own servers. Self-hosting is available for those who want more control or lower long-term costs, but it is an option rather than a requirement.
