What is Make.com?

Make.com is a cloud-based visual automation platform that allows individuals, freelancers, and teams to connect apps and automate multi-step workflows without writing code. Originally launched as Integromat in 2012 and rebranded as Make in 2022, it is headquartered in Prague and serves over 500,000 users worldwide. Its defining feature is a drag-and-drop scenario canvas that makes complex, branching automations far more approachable than traditional code-based solutions.

Who is Make.com best for?

Make.com is best suited to technically minded users, developers, and small-to-mid-size teams who need powerful, flexible automation at a lower cost than Zapier. It is less well suited to complete beginners who want a simple, linear "if this then that" setup, or to large enterprises that require enterprise-grade SLAs and dedicated account management out of the box.

  • Developers and technical freelancers — who want fine-grained control over data transformation, HTTP requests, and custom API calls without leaving a visual interface.
  • Small marketing and operations teams — looking to build complex, multi-branch workflows connecting CRM, email, Slack, spreadsheets, and e-commerce tools at a price that scales sensibly.
  • Agencies and consultants — who manage automations for multiple clients and need a platform flexible enough to handle varied tech stacks without paying per-seat Zapier prices.

Key features of Make.com

Below are the capabilities that define Make.com's core offering and distinguish it from competing tools in the automation space.

  • Visual scenario builder — a drag-and-drop canvas where each app or action is a module; connections between modules are drawn as lines, making the full data flow visible at a glance.
  • 1,500+ app integrations — pre-built connectors to popular tools including Google Workspace, Slack, Airtable, Shopify, HubSpot, Notion, and OpenAI, with new integrations added regularly.
  • Advanced data transformation — built-in functions for parsing, mapping, formatting, and aggregating data between steps, far more capable than Zapier's equivalent out of the box.
  • Conditional routing and filters — routers that split a scenario into multiple branches based on field values, enabling genuinely complex if/else logic without workarounds.
  • Error handling and rollback — dedicated error-handler routes and automatic rollback for incomplete scenario runs, reducing the risk of partial data writes.
  • HTTP / webhook module — a universal module for calling any REST API or receiving webhooks, effectively making Make.com compatible with any web service regardless of whether a native integration exists.

Pros and cons of Make.com

Based on several months of hands-on testing across automation, data-sync, and API-integration scenarios, here is our balanced assessment of Make.com's strengths and weaknesses.

Pros

  • Highly visual canvas makes complex workflows easy to audit and debug
  • Best-in-class data transformation without requiring code
  • Significantly cheaper than Zapier at equivalent operation volumes
  • Generous free tier suitable for personal projects and prototyping
  • HTTP module enables integration with virtually any REST API

Cons

  • Steeper learning curve than Zapier — the canvas can feel overwhelming for beginners
  • Operation-based pricing is harder to predict than task-based models
  • Fewer native integrations than Zapier (1,500+ vs 6,000+)
  • Execution history retention is limited on lower-tier plans
  • Mobile experience is view-only; scenario editing requires a desktop browser

FAQ: Make.com

The most common questions readers ask about Make.com, answered directly.

What is Make.com?

Make.com is a cloud-based visual automation platform that lets users connect apps and automate multi-step workflows using a drag-and-drop canvas, without writing code.

How much does Make.com cost?

Make.com offers a free plan and paid plans starting at $9 per month (billed annually) for the Core tier, scaling up to $29 per month for the Teams plan; Enterprise pricing is custom.

Is there a free plan for Make.com?

Yes — Make.com's free plan includes 1,000 operations per month, up to two active scenarios, and a minimum 5-minute execution interval, with no credit card required.

What are the main features of Make.com?

Make.com's core features include a visual scenario builder, 1,500+ app integrations, advanced data transformation functions, conditional routing, error handling with rollback, and a universal HTTP/webhook module.

Who is Make.com best for?

Make.com is best suited to technically minded users, developers, and small-to-mid-size teams who need powerful, flexible automation at a lower cost than Zapier.

What are the pros of Make.com?

The main advantages of Make.com are its highly visual workflow canvas, best-in-class data transformation, competitive pricing, and a generous free tier that is suitable for prototyping and personal projects.

What are the cons of Make.com?

The main drawbacks of Make.com are a steeper learning curve than Zapier, an operation-based pricing model that can be hard to predict, and fewer native integrations than competing platforms.

How does Make.com compare to Zapier?

Make.com is more powerful and affordable than Zapier for complex, branching workflows, but Zapier is easier to set up for simple linear automations and has a significantly larger native integration library; see our Make.com vs Zapier comparison for a full breakdown.

Is Make.com worth it?

Make.com is worth it for technically minded users and teams who need advanced automation logic at a competitive price, but may be overkill for those who only need simple, two-step automations.

Does Make.com offer a free trial?

Make.com does not offer a time-limited free trial, but its permanent free plan lets you test core functionality with 1,000 operations per month and two active scenarios at no cost and with no credit card required.

Our verdict on Make.com

Make.com is one of the most capable automation platforms available in 2026, particularly for users who need complex, multi-branch workflows with serious data-transformation requirements. Its visual canvas is genuinely impressive, and its pricing undercuts Zapier significantly at equivalent operation volumes. The main trade-off is a learning curve that will slow down complete beginners — but for anyone with a basic understanding of APIs and data flows, Make.com delivers exceptional value. If your team is more technical and wants deeper control, n8n Cloud is worth considering as an alternative.

Which should you choose?

Make.com is usually the best fit for business users, marketers, and teams that want fast prototyping, a visual builder, and a large template library with minimal technical overhead.

n8n Cloud is the better choice for developers and technical teams who want maximum control, custom code nodes, and support for complex AI and agentic workflows. Read our n8n Cloud Review (2026) or compare both tools side by side in our Make vs n8n comparison.
